# Customer Risk Assessment

## Assessment Summary

This assessment evaluates the prospective customer’s AML/KYC risk based on customer, business, geographic, product, transaction, source-of-funds, sanctions, PEP and adverse-media factors.

The customer is a Namibian business owner seeking to open an account with a South African fintech company for cross-border supplier payments.

## Preliminary Overall Risk Rating

**Moderate Risk**

The customer’s profile appears commercially plausible, but the cross-border nature of the relationship, use of digital financial services, and incomplete verification and screening justify a moderate preliminary risk rating.

## Risk Mitigation Measures

The following controls are recommended:

- Verify the customer’s identity and residential address
- Confirm the business registration and beneficial ownership
- Review bank statements against stated monthly turnover
- Validate supplier invoices and expected counterparties
- Conduct sanctions, PEP and adverse-media screening
- Apply transaction monitoring after onboarding
- Review activity that falls outside the expected customer profile
- Conduct a periodic customer review
- Escalate unusual or unexplained activity for further investigation

## Preliminary Recommendation

The customer may be considered for onboarding only after all outstanding verification and screening steps have been completed satisfactorily.

If no material sanctions, PEP, adverse-media or source-of-funds concerns are identified, the customer may be approved as a moderate-risk customer subject to ongoing monitoring.
