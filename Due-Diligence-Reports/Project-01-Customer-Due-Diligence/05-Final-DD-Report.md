# Final Customer Due Diligence Report

## 1. Executive Summary

This report presents the final customer due diligence assessment of Daniel Amutenya, a fictional Namibian business owner seeking to open an account with a South African fintech company for cross-border supplier payments.

The assessment considered:

- Customer identity and verification
- Business registration and ownership
- Nature and purpose of the relationship
- Source of funds and source of wealth
- Expected transaction activity
- Geographic and cross-border risk
- Sanctions screening
- Politically exposed person screening
- Adverse media screening
- Overall customer risk

Based on the information reviewed, the customer presents a **moderate AML/KYC risk**.

No simulated sanctions, PEP, adverse-media or material name-matching concerns were identified.

The stated account purpose appears commercially reasonable and consistent with the customer’s retail business. However, final approval should remain subject to satisfactory completion of identity, address, business-registration, beneficial-ownership and source-of-funds verification.

## 5. Business and Beneficial Ownership Verification

The customer stated that he is the sole owner and beneficial owner of Amutenya Retail Traders.

The ownership structure appears simple and transparent. However, the business registration and beneficial ownership must be independently confirmed.

**Assessment:** Partially complete

**Required action:** Verify the business registration and beneficial ownership against official registry or equivalent records.

## 6. Nature and Purpose of the Relationship

The customer intends to use the account to pay South African suppliers for stock purchased for resale in Namibia.

This purpose is commercially plausible and appears consistent with the stated retail business.

The expected monthly turnover of N$80,000 to N$120,000 appears reasonable, subject to confirmation against bank statements, supplier invoices and future account activity.

**Assessment:** Reasonable and consistent with the customer profile

## 7. Source of Funds and Source of Wealth

The customer stated that account funding will originate from revenue generated by the retail business.

Three months of business bank statements and supplier invoices were provided. These documents appear relevant, but detailed verification is required to confirm that the transaction activity is consistent with the stated business operations and turnover.

The stated source of wealth is ownership of the retail business.

**Assessment:** Plausible but not fully verified

**Required actions:**

- Review incoming credits and business revenue patterns
- Compare turnover with the stated expected activity
- Review supplier invoices and counterparties
- Investigate any unexplained third-party payments
- Obtain further supporting evidence if material inconsistencies are identified

## 12. Recommendation

**Recommended Decision:** Conditional Approval

The customer may be approved for onboarding only after the following requirements have been completed satisfactorily:

- Verify the customer’s identity document
- Verify the residential address
- Confirm the business registration
- Confirm beneficial ownership
- Complete the bank-statement review
- Validate the stated source of funds
- Confirm relevant supplier invoices and expected counterparties
- Retain evidence of sanctions, PEP and adverse-media screening

If the outstanding checks are completed without identifying material concerns, the customer may be onboarded as a moderate-risk customer.

## 13. Post-Onboarding Controls

The following controls are recommended:

- Apply transaction monitoring
- Compare actual activity with the expected customer profile
- Review unexplained third-party payments
- Review activity above expected turnover
- Monitor changes in jurisdictions, counterparties or payment patterns
- Conduct periodic customer reviews
- Refresh screening and customer information when required
- Escalate unusual or unexplained activity for further investigation

## 14. Final Conclusion

The customer’s stated business activity and account purpose appear reasonable and commercially plausible.

The main risk arises from the cross-border nature of the relationship, use of a digital financial platform, and incomplete verification of supporting information.

No simulated screening concerns were identified.

The appropriate outcome is therefore **conditional approval as a moderate-risk customer**, subject to completion of the outstanding verification and source-of-funds checks.
