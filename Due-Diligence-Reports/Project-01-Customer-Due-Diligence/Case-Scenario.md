# Case Scenario

## Background

A South African fintech company has received an application from a prospective customer who wishes to open a business account for cross-border commercial payments.

The customer is a Namibian resident and the owner of a retail business based in Windhoek. The account will primarily be used to pay South African suppliers for stock purchased for resale in Namibia.

## Customer Details

**Customer name:** Daniel Amutenya  
**Nationality:** Namibian  
**Country of residence:** Namibia  
**Occupation:** Business owner  
**Business name:** Amutenya Retail Traders  
**Business location:** Windhoek, Namibia  
**Business activity:** Retail sale of household and consumer goods  
**Account purpose:** Payments to South African suppliers  
**Expected monthly turnover:** N$80,000 to N$120,000  
**Expected transaction type:** Electronic cross-border supplier payments  
**Expected jurisdictions:** Namibia and South Africa  

## Documents Provided

The customer submitted the following documents:

- Certified copy of Namibian identity document
- Recent proof of residential address
- Business registration certificate
- Tax registration document
- Three months of business bank statements
- Supplier invoices
- Brief explanation of the source of funds

## Initial Risk Considerations

The following factors require assessment:

- The customer is involved in cross-border transactions
- The account will be used for commercial payments
- The expected turnover is moderate
- The business operates in the retail sector
- The relationship involves Namibia and South Africa
- The source of funds must be verified
- The customer must be screened for sanctions, PEP exposure and adverse media
- The expected transaction activity must be compared with the customer profile

## Due Diligence Objective

The purpose of the assessment is to determine:

1. Whether the customer’s identity and business can be verified
2. Whether the stated source of funds is reasonable and supported
3. Whether the intended account activity is consistent with the business profile
4. Whether any sanctions, PEP or adverse media concerns exist
5. The overall customer risk rating
6. Whether the customer should be approved, rejected or referred for enhanced due diligence
