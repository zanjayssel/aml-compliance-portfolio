# Red-Flag Assessment

## 1. Purpose of Assessment

This assessment identifies and evaluates potential AML red flags arising from the fictional customer’s transaction activity.

The objective is not to conclude that criminal activity occurred, but to determine whether the activity is inconsistent with the customer profile and whether further investigation or escalation is justified.

## 2. Red-Flag Rating Scale

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Low | Activity appears broadly consistent with the customer profile |
| Moderate | Activity requires clarification or supporting documentation |
| High | Activity is materially inconsistent with the customer profile and warrants escalation |

## 4. Detailed Red-Flag Analysis

### 4.1 Activity Exceeding the Expected Customer Profile

The customer stated that monthly turnover would range between N$80,000 and N$120,000.

The account received N$218,550 during the review period.

This exceeded the upper limit of the expected range by N$98,550.

The excess activity is significant and requires explanation, particularly because a large portion of the incoming funds did not appear to originate from ordinary retail sales.

**Risk Rating:** High

### 4.2 Third-Party Payments

The account received funds from:

- K. Shikongo
- M. Amutenya
- Blue Rock Trading

These senders were not identified as expected customers, suppliers or counterparties during onboarding.

The payments were described as:

- Loan
- Family support
- Settlement

These explanations do not clearly relate to the customer’s stated retail-business operations.

**Risk Rating:** High

### 4.3 Rapid Movement of Funds

Three incoming payments were followed by outgoing payments on the same day:

- N$30,000 received, followed by N$29,500 transferred out
- N$50,000 received, followed by N$50,000 transferred out
- N$42,000 received, followed by N$40,000 transferred out

The rapid onward transfer of almost the full amount may indicate that the account is being used as a conduit.

**Risk Rating:** High

### 4.4 Possible Pass-Through Activity

The three transaction sequences involved a combined N$122,000 in incoming funds and N$119,500 in outgoing payments.

Approximately 98% of the incoming funds were transferred onward on the same respective days.

This pattern is inconsistent with the expected operation of a retail business account, where funds would ordinarily be retained or used for identifiable business expenses.

**Risk Rating:** High

### 4.5 Unexpected Jurisdictions

The customer stated that expected activity would involve Namibia and South Africa.

The account also showed transactions involving:

- Botswana
- Mauritius
- United Arab Emirates

These jurisdictions were not disclosed during onboarding.

The geographic expansion is particularly concerning because it was linked to rapid fund movement and unclear transaction purposes.

**Risk Rating:** High

### 4.6 Digital-Asset-Related Payment

TX010 involved a N$50,000 payment to Global Digital Assets in the United Arab Emirates.

The payment:

- Followed receipt of N$50,000 from a family member
- Occurred on the same day
- Was unrelated to the customer’s retail-business purpose
- Involved a digital-asset-related counterparty
- Fell outside the expected geographic profile

The transaction requires enhanced review and supporting documentation.

**Risk Rating:** High

### 4.7 Repeated Round-Number Payments

Three payments of N$10,000 each were made to different individuals on the same day.

The payments shared the same general description: “Business services”.

The repeated values and vague descriptions may indicate transaction structuring, coordinated payments or unsupported expenditure.

Legitimate explanations remain possible, but documentary evidence is required.

**Risk Rating:** Moderate to High

### 4.8 Payments to Individuals

The customer was expected to make payments mainly to South African wholesalers and suppliers.

Three payments were instead made to individuals.

The relationship between the customer and the recipients is unknown.

**Risk Rating:** Moderate

### 4.9 Vague Transaction References

Several transactions used unclear references, including:

- Consulting fee
- Business services
- Settlement
- Consultancy

These references do not provide enough information to establish a clear commercial purpose.

**Risk Rating:** Moderate

### 4.10 Activity Unrelated to the Stated Business Purpose

The customer stated that the account would mainly be used to pay South African suppliers for retail stock.

The following activity appears unrelated to that purpose:

- Personal loan receipt
- Family-support receipt
- Digital-asset investment
- Foreign settlement
- Consultancy payment
- Repeated payments to individuals

The volume and frequency of unrelated activity indicate that the account may be used for purposes beyond those disclosed at onboarding.

**Risk Rating:** High

## 5. Red-Flag Interaction

The concerns should not be viewed in isolation.

The risk increases because several indicators occur together:

- Third-party payments
- Same-day onward transfers
- Unexpected jurisdictions
- Vague references
- Digital-asset exposure
- Turnover exceeding the expected profile
- Activity unrelated to the stated business purpose

The combination of these factors creates a stronger basis for escalation than any single transaction would create on its own.

## 6. Information Required

The following information should be requested:

- Loan agreement relating to TX004
- Identity and relationship of K. Shikongo
- Invoice and service agreement supporting TX005
- Identity and relationship of M. Amutenya
- Explanation for the use of the business account for family funds
- Evidence relating to the digital-asset payment
- Contracts and invoices for TX012, TX013 and TX014
- Explanation for the identical payment values
- Settlement agreement supporting TX017
- Consultancy agreement and invoice supporting TX018
- Corporate information and beneficial ownership details for the foreign counterparties
- Updated explanation of the customer’s expected account activity

## 7. Escalation Assessment

The activity presents multiple high-risk indicators and material deviations from the expected customer profile.

The available information is insufficient to establish a legitimate commercial explanation for the flagged transactions.

**Escalation Decision:** Escalation is warranted

The case should be referred to the appropriate AML or compliance function for enhanced investigation.

## 8. Recommended Interim Controls

Pending the outcome of the investigation, the following controls should be considered:

- Enhanced monitoring of the account
- Review of further transactions involving the same counterparties
- Updated customer due diligence
- Reassessment of the customer risk rating
- Screening of all relevant counterparties
- Restriction or review of transactions outside the expected profile, where permitted by policy and law
- Documentation of all customer explanations and supporting evidence

## 9. Conclusion

The reviewed activity contains multiple material AML red flags.

The strongest concerns relate to:

- Rapid movement of funds
- Possible pass-through activity
- Third-party payments
- Unexpected jurisdictions
- Digital-asset-related activity
- Repeated round-number payments
- Activity inconsistent with the stated account purpose

The appropriate next step is enhanced investigation and formal internal escalation.
