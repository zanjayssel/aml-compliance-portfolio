# Customer Profile

## 1. Customer Overview

This fictional customer profile forms the basis of the transaction-monitoring review.

The customer is a Namibian retail business owner who was previously onboarded as a moderate-risk customer.

## 2. Customer Details

**Customer name:** Daniel Amutenya  
**Nationality:** Namibian  
**Country of residence:** Namibia  
**Occupation:** Business owner  
**Business name:** Amutenya Retail Traders  
**Business location:** Windhoek, Namibia  
**Business activity:** Retail sale of household and consumer goods  
**Customer risk rating:** Moderate Risk  

## 3. Account Purpose

The customer stated that the account would mainly be used to make payments to South African suppliers for stock purchased for resale in Namibia.

## 4. Expected Account Activity

The following activity was expected at onboarding:

| Category | Expected Activity |
|---|---|
| Monthly turnover | N$80,000 to N$120,000 |
| Main transaction type | Electronic supplier payments |
| Main counterparties | South African wholesalers and suppliers |
| Main jurisdictions | Namibia and South Africa |
| Expected incoming funds | Retail business revenue |
| Expected outgoing funds | Supplier payments and ordinary business expenses |
| Expected transaction frequency | Regular commercial payments throughout the month |
| Expected payment values | Generally consistent with supplier invoices and business turnover |

## 5. Expected Source of Funds

The customer stated that incoming funds would originate from retail sales made through Amutenya Retail Traders.

The expected source of funds was therefore ordinary business revenue.

## 6. Expected Source of Wealth

The customer stated that his source of wealth was ownership and operation of the retail business.

## 7. Expected Transaction Behaviour

The account was expected to show:

- Regular incoming payments linked to retail sales
- Payments to identifiable suppliers
- Transaction values consistent with the size of the business
- Commercial activity involving Namibia and South Africa
- No unexplained third-party transfers
- No rapid movement of funds without an apparent business purpose
- No significant activity involving unrelated high-risk jurisdictions
- No unexplained cash-like or personal transactions

## 8. Monitoring Triggers

The following activity would require further review:

- Monthly turnover significantly exceeding the expected range
- Payments to unrelated individuals
- Multiple third-party deposits
- Rapid movement of funds shortly after receipt
- Repeated round-number transfers
- Payments to jurisdictions outside the expected profile
- Transaction descriptions inconsistent with retail activity
- Sudden changes in transaction volume or frequency
- Supplier payments unsupported by invoices
- Activity inconsistent with the customer’s known business

## 9. Monitoring Objective

The objective of the review is to compare actual account activity with this expected customer profile and determine whether:

1. The transactions are consistent with the stated business purpose
2. Any unusual patterns require clarification
3. Further customer information should be requested
4. The activity should be escalated for investigation
5. The customer risk rating should be updated
