# Final Case Conclusion

## 1. Case Summary

This fictional transaction-monitoring case reviewed the activity of Daniel Amutenya and Amutenya Retail Traders during May 2026.

The customer had originally been onboarded as a moderate-risk retail business customer with an expected monthly turnover of N$80,000 to N$120,000.

The stated account purpose was to receive retail-business revenue and make payments to suppliers in Namibia and South Africa.

The review identified several transactions that were consistent with the customer profile, including ordinary retail receipts, supplier payments, rent and logistics expenses.

However, the review also identified material activity that did not fit the expected profile.

## 2. Main Findings

The following concerns were identified:

- Total incoming activity exceeded the expected monthly range
- N$122,000 of incoming funds did not appear to relate to ordinary retail sales
- Three same-day transaction sequences displayed possible pass-through characteristics
- Approximately 98% of the unusual incoming funds were transferred onward on the same respective days
- Large third-party payments were received
- Transactions involved unexpected jurisdictions
- A payment was made to a digital-asset-related counterparty
- Repeated round-number payments were made to individuals
- Several payment descriptions were vague
- Supporting documentation was not available
- The account appeared to be used for purposes beyond those disclosed at onboarding

## 3. Activity Consistent with the Customer Profile

The review did not find all account activity to be unusual.

The following activity appeared broadly consistent with the customer’s stated business purpose:

- Retail receipts totalling N$96,550
- Supplier payments to South African wholesalers
- Commercial rent
- Logistics expenses

This ordinary activity supports the existence of genuine retail-business operations.

However, the presence of legitimate activity does not resolve the concerns arising from the unusual transactions.

## 4. Key Risk Determination

The customer’s risk profile changed materially during the review period.

The combination of:

- Third-party funding
- Rapid movement of funds
- Possible pass-through activity
- Unexpected jurisdictions
- Digital-asset exposure
- Unclear counterparties
- Round-number payments
- Activity unrelated to the stated business purpose

created a level of risk higher than the moderate rating assigned at onboarding.

## 5. Final Risk Rating

**Original risk rating:** Moderate Risk

**Revised interim risk rating:** High Risk Pending Investigation

The higher rating is justified because the available information does not provide a clear and supported commercial explanation for the unusual activity.

## 6. Final Decision

**Decision:** Escalate for Enhanced Investigation

The case should not be closed as ordinary activity without further review.

The customer should be asked to provide explanations and supporting documents for the flagged transactions.

Relevant counterparties should be screened and independently researched.

The customer’s account purpose, expected transaction activity and source-of-funds profile should also be updated.

## 7. Recommended Actions

The following actions are recommended:

1. Obtain customer explanations for all flagged transactions
2. Request loan, settlement, consultancy, service and investment documentation
3. Verify the identities and relationships of third-party senders and recipients
4. Conduct sanctions, PEP and adverse-media screening
5. Verify corporate registration and beneficial ownership of relevant entities
6. Review additional transaction history
7. Apply enhanced monitoring
8. Update the customer risk assessment
9. Assess whether the activity has a reasonable commercial explanation
10. Consider whether external reporting obligations arise under the applicable legal framework

## 8. Reporting Position

This portfolio case does not make a formal determination that a suspicious transaction report must be filed.

A reporting decision would depend on:

- The customer’s explanation
- Supporting documentation
- Screening results
- Additional transaction history
- Internal policy
- Applicable law
- The judgement of the designated AML or compliance officer

The correct outcome at this stage is therefore internal escalation rather than a definitive conclusion of criminal conduct.

## 9. Final Analytical Conclusion

The transaction activity contained multiple material deviations from the expected customer profile.

The strongest concerns involved rapid same-day movement of funds, third-party transfers, unexpected jurisdictions, vague transaction references and activity unrelated to the customer’s stated retail purpose.

The activity may have a legitimate explanation, but the available information is insufficient to support one.

The case should therefore remain open for enhanced investigation, and the customer should be treated as high risk pending the outcome.
