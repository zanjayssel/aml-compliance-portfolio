# Internal Escalation Report

## 1. Case Information

**Customer:** Daniel Amutenya  
**Business:** Amutenya Retail Traders  
**Customer risk rating at onboarding:** Moderate Risk  
**Review period:** 2 May 2026 to 30 May 2026  
**Case type:** Transaction monitoring escalation  
**Escalation status:** Further investigation required  

## 2. Reason for Escalation

The customer’s account activity materially deviated from the expected profile recorded during onboarding.

The customer stated that the account would primarily be used for retail-business revenue and payments to South African suppliers.

The review identified:

- Incoming activity exceeding the expected monthly turnover
- Large third-party transfers
- Same-day onward movement of funds
- Possible pass-through activity
- Transactions involving unexpected jurisdictions
- A digital-asset-related payment
- Repeated round-number payments to individuals
- Vague transaction descriptions
- Activity unrelated to the stated retail-business purpose

The combined value, frequency and pattern of the unusual transactions justify formal internal escalation.

## 3. Expected Customer Activity

The customer’s expected account profile included:

- Monthly turnover of N$80,000 to N$120,000
- Incoming funds from retail sales
- Outgoing payments to suppliers
- Main activity involving Namibia and South Africa
- Transaction behaviour consistent with a retail business

## 4. Actual Account Activity

A total of 20 transactions were reviewed.

| Category | Total |
|---|---:|
| Incoming transactions | N$218,550 |
| Outgoing transactions | N$247,000 |
| Total account activity | N$465,550 |
| Stated retail receipts | N$96,550 |
| Unusual incoming funds | N$122,000 |
| Same-day onward transfers linked to unusual receipts | N$119,500 |

The total incoming activity exceeded the customer’s stated maximum expected monthly turnover by N$98,550.

## 5. Key Transactions

### Transaction Sequence 1

**TX004:** N$30,000 received from K. Shikongo  
**Reference:** Loan  

**TX005:** N$29,500 paid to Africa Trade Solutions on the same day  
**Reference:** Consulting fee  

**Concern:** Nearly the full amount received was transferred onward immediately, with no clear connection to the customer’s retail business.

### Transaction Sequence 2

**TX009:** N$50,000 received from M. Amutenya  
**Reference:** Family support  

**TX010:** N$50,000 paid to Global Digital Assets in the United Arab Emirates on the same day  
**Reference:** Investment  

**Concern:** The full amount was moved to a digital-asset-related counterparty in an unexpected jurisdiction and was unrelated to the stated business purpose.

### Transaction Sequence 3

**TX012, TX013 and TX014:** Three payments of N$10,000 each to separate individuals on the same day  
**Reference:** Business services  

**Concern:** Identical round-number payments, vague descriptions and individual recipients require supporting evidence.

### Transaction Sequence 4

**TX017:** N$42,000 received from Blue Rock Trading in Botswana  
**Reference:** Settlement  

**TX018:** N$40,000 paid to North Star Holdings in Mauritius on the same day  
**Reference:** Consultancy  

**Concern:** Rapid cross-border movement involving undisclosed counterparties and jurisdictions outside the expected customer profile.

## 9. Risk Assessment

The activity presents a higher level of risk than was identified at onboarding.

The customer’s current moderate-risk rating may no longer be appropriate because:

- The account is being used beyond its stated purpose
- Undisclosed counterparties and jurisdictions are involved
- Significant funds were rapidly transferred onward
- Transaction explanations are vague
- The available information does not establish a clear commercial rationale

**Recommended interim risk rating:** High Risk Pending Investigation

## 10. Escalation Recommendation

The case should be escalated to the appropriate AML or compliance decision-maker for enhanced investigation.

Recommended actions:

1. Request customer clarification and supporting documentation
2. Screen all relevant counterparties
3. Conduct enhanced due diligence
4. Review additional account activity
5. Update the customer risk assessment
6. Apply enhanced monitoring
7. Assess whether the activity has a reasonable and supported commercial explanation
8. Consider whether external reporting obligations arise under the applicable legal and regulatory framework

No conclusion should be reached regarding criminal conduct until the investigation has been completed.

## 11. Internal Escalation Narrative

Transaction monitoring identified material activity inconsistent with the customer’s stated retail-business profile.

During May 2026, the account received N$218,550, exceeding the stated maximum expected monthly turnover of N$120,000 by N$98,550. Of the total incoming funds, N$122,000 originated from a personal loan, family support and a foreign settlement rather than ordinary retail revenue.

Three transaction sequences showed same-day onward movement of approximately 98% of the funds received. These included a payment to a digital-asset-related counterparty in the United Arab Emirates and a separate payment to a consultancy-related entity in Mauritius.

The account also made three identical N$10,000 payments to individuals using the vague reference “Business services”.

The activity involved undisclosed counterparties, unexpected jurisdictions, rapid movement of funds and transactions unrelated to the customer’s stated account purpose.

Further investigation, customer clarification, counterparty screening and enhanced due diligence are recommended.

## 12. Final Escalation Decision

**Decision:** Escalate for enhanced investigation

**Interim customer risk rating:** High Risk Pending Investigation

**Recommended monitoring status:** Enhanced Monitoring
