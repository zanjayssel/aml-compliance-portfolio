# AML Program Remediation Recommendations

## 1. Purpose

This document sets out practical remediation recommendations arising from the NorthBridge Pay, Inc. AML Policy Gap Analysis.

The recommendations are designed to help management:

- Strengthen the written AML policy
- Translate policy requirements into operational procedures
- Improve consistency and accountability
- Address material financial-crime risks
- Prepare the compliance framework for sponsor-bank and payment-processor review

## 2. Remediation Principles

NorthBridge Pay should apply the following principles throughout the remediation programme:

1. Controls should be proportionate to the company’s products, customers, jurisdictions, transaction volumes and delivery channels.
2. Responsibilities should be clearly assigned.
3. Decisions should be documented and supported by evidence.
4. High-risk activity should receive increased scrutiny.
5. Policies should be supported by procedures, templates, logs and quality controls.
6. Third-party tools should support the programme but should not replace internal oversight.
7. Remediation should be tracked until independently validated and formally closed.

## 3. Governance and Oversight

### Recommendation

Strengthen the governance section of the AML policy by clearly defining the responsibilities of:

- The board or governing authority
- Senior management
- The Chief Compliance Officer
- Compliance analysts
- Operations staff
- Technology and product teams
- Third-party service providers

### Required Controls

NorthBridge Pay should:

- Give the CCO sufficient authority, independence and access to information
- Require the CCO to report material issues directly to senior management
- Establish a formal board or management reporting schedule
- Document approval of the AML policy and material changes
- Record unresolved risks, overdue remediation and significant compliance incidents
- Ensure that sufficient staffing and resources are provided

### Suggested Evidence

- Approved governance charter
- CCO job description
- Compliance reporting calendar
- Board and management minutes
- Resource and staffing assessment
- AML issue-escalation procedure

## 4. Enterprise AML Risk Assessment

### Recommendation

Complete and maintain a documented enterprise-wide AML risk assessment.

### Required Risk Categories

The assessment should consider:

- Customer risk
- Product and service risk
- Geographic risk
- Transaction risk
- Delivery-channel risk
- Crypto-related risk
- Third-party and vendor risk
- Fraud and account-takeover exposure

### Required Process

NorthBridge Pay should:

1. Identify inherent risks
2. Assess existing controls
3. Determine residual risk
4. Record the rationale for each rating
5. Obtain management approval
6. Review the assessment at least annually
7. Update it following material changes

### Trigger Events

An interim update should be considered when the company:

- Launches a new product
- Enters a new jurisdiction
- Adds a new payment channel
- Changes its sponsor bank or processor
- Introduces additional crypto functionality
- Experiences significant control failures
- Identifies new typologies or regulatory concerns

## 5. Customer Identification and Verification

### Recommendation

Create clear customer identification and verification procedures for individuals and businesses.

### Individual Customers

The procedure should define:

- Required identifying information
- Acceptable identity documents
- Address-verification methods
- Liveness or biometric requirements where used
- Handling of expired or altered documents
- Failed and inconclusive verification results
- Manual review requirements
- Exception approval

### Business Customers

The procedure should define:

- Required formation and registration documents
- Tax-identification information
- Business address
- Nature of business
- Ownership and control information
- Authorised representatives
- Expected account purpose
- Verification through reliable and independent sources

### Failed Verification

Accounts should not be activated until required verification is completed, unless a documented and authorised exception is permitted by policy and applicable requirements.

## 6. Customer Due Diligence

### Recommendation

Introduce a mandatory minimum CDD standard for every customer.

### Minimum CDD Information

The company should obtain and record:

- Customer identity
- Nature and purpose of the relationship
- Occupation or business activity
- Expected transaction types
- Expected transaction values and frequency
- Expected jurisdictions
- Expected counterparties
- Source of funds where relevant
- Ownership and control information
- Initial customer risk rating

### Customer Acceptance

The policy should state:

- Which customers may be approved automatically
- Which customers require manual review
- Which customers require CCO or senior approval
- Which customer types are prohibited
- When the relationship must be rejected or exited

## 7. Customer Risk-Rating Methodology

### Recommendation

Implement a documented and repeatable customer-risk model.

### Suggested Risk Factors

The model should consider:

- Customer type
- Business or occupation
- Products used
- Geographic exposure
- Transaction profile
- Delivery channel
- Ownership complexity
- PEP status
- Sanctions exposure
- Adverse media
- Source-of-funds concerns
- Crypto exposure

### Risk Categories

Customers should be classified as:

- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk
- Prohibited or Outside Risk Appetite

### Governance

The methodology should define:

- Factor weightings
- Score thresholds
- Override authority
- Required rationale
- Approval requirements
- Review frequency
- Escalation of material changes

## 8. Enhanced Due Diligence

### Recommendation

Define specific EDD triggers and required actions.

### Suggested EDD Triggers

EDD should be considered for:

- PEPs and relevant family members or close associates
- Higher-risk jurisdictions
- Complex or opaque ownership structures
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Unusual or high-volume cross-border activity
- Crypto-related businesses or transactions
- Significant adverse media
- Unexplained third-party funding
- Source-of-funds inconsistencies
- Customers whose activity materially differs from their profile

### EDD Measures

EDD may include:

- Additional identity verification
- Independent corporate research
- Beneficial-ownership verification
- Source-of-funds evidence
- Source-of-wealth evidence
- Adverse-media research
- Senior-management approval
- Increased monitoring
- More frequent periodic review

## 9. Beneficial Ownership

### Recommendation

Create detailed procedures for identifying and verifying natural persons who own or control business customers.

### Required Controls

The procedure should address:

- Direct ownership
- Indirect ownership
- Ownership through legal entities
- Control through voting rights
- Control through management or other means
- Nominee arrangements
- Trusts and similar structures
- Complex or layered ownership
- Inconsistencies between documents and customer statements

### Verification

Ownership information should be compared with reliable records and supporting documents.

Unresolved discrepancies should be escalated before onboarding.

## 10. Sanctions Screening

### Recommendation

Adopt a documented, risk-based sanctions compliance framework.

### Screening Events

Screening should occur:

- At onboarding
- Before account activation
- When customer details change
- Periodically or continuously, depending on the system
- Before or during relevant payment processing
- When sanctions lists are updated
- When a possible related party is identified

### Alert Handling

The procedure should require:

1. Review of identifying data
2. Comparison with the listed party
3. Documentation of match strength
4. False-positive rationale
5. Escalation of potential true matches
6. Restriction or blocking actions where legally required
7. Preservation of screening evidence

### Vendor Oversight

NorthBridge Pay should test whether the screening tool:

- Uses relevant lists
- Refreshes data promptly
- Detects aliases and spelling variations
- Retains alert records
- Supports auditable decision-making

## 11. PEP Controls

### Recommendation

Define domestic and foreign PEPs, family members and close associates.

### Required Measures

For relevant PEP relationships, the company should consider:

- Enhanced due diligence
- Senior-management approval
- Source-of-wealth assessment
- Source-of-funds assessment
- Adverse-media review
- Enhanced transaction monitoring
- More frequent periodic review
- Documented risk rationale

PEP status should not automatically mean rejection. Decisions should be risk-based and documented.

## 12. Transaction Monitoring

### Recommendation

Document the design and operation of the transaction-monitoring framework.

### Required Documentation

For each monitoring rule, the company should record:

- The risk or typology addressed
- Relevant products and customers
- Thresholds and parameters
- Data sources
- Alert frequency
- Expected false-positive rate
- Investigation steps
- Escalation criteria
- Rule owner
- Review and tuning frequency

### Alert Investigation Standards

Analysts should:

1. Review the customer profile
2. Review relevant transaction history
3. Identify counterparties and jurisdictions
4. Compare actual and expected activity
5. Review prior alerts and cases
6. Request supporting information where appropriate
7. Document findings
8. Decide whether to close or escalate

### Quality Controls

The company should introduce:

- Sample review of closed alerts
- Escalation-quality checks
- Rule-performance monitoring
- Backlog and ageing reports
- Tuning and validation
- Documentation of system changes

## 13. Crypto-Related Controls

### Recommendation

Develop specific controls for crypto-related transactions and counterparties.

### Risk Areas

The framework should consider:

- Rapid fiat-to-crypto conversion
- High-risk or unregistered exchanges
- Mixers or tumblers
- Privacy-enhancing services
- Sanctioned wallet exposure
- High-risk wallet clusters
- Multiple third-party deposits
- Rapid movement between fiat and crypto
- Transactions inconsistent with the customer profile

### Controls

NorthBridge Pay should consider:

- Crypto-specific customer profiling
- Counterparty screening
- Blockchain-analytics support where appropriate
- Enhanced monitoring rules
- Escalation criteria
- Limits or restrictions based on risk
- Documentation of vendor capabilities

## 14. Suspicious-Activity Investigation

### Recommendation

Implement a formal investigation and internal-escalation procedure.

### Investigation Stages

The procedure should include:

1. Alert generation or referral
2. Initial review
3. Evidence collection
4. Customer-profile comparison
5. Counterparty and geographic analysis
6. Customer clarification where appropriate
7. Red-flag assessment
8. Internal escalation
9. Decision and approval
10. Case closure and retention

### Case Documentation

Each case should contain:

- Case identifier
- Customer details
- Review period
- Triggering activity
- Transaction summary
- Red flags
- Evidence reviewed
- Customer explanation
- Screening results
- Analyst conclusion
- Escalation decision
- Reviewer approval
- Follow-up actions

## 15. Suspicious Activity Reporting Governance

### Recommendation

Create a separate internal procedure for determining whether an external suspicious-activity report is required where applicable.

Certain MSBs, including money transmitters, are subject to FinCEN suspicious-activity reporting requirements. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

### Required Controls

The procedure should address:

- Decision authority
- Applicable reporting thresholds
- Filing deadlines
- Supporting documentation
- SAR confidentiality
- Quality review
- Electronic filing
- Continuing-activity review
- Record retention
- Documentation of decisions not to report

The policy should distinguish between:

- Internal escalation
- Enhanced investigation
- External reporting
- Account restriction or exit decisions

## 16. Recordkeeping

### Recommendation

Create a formal records-retention schedule.

### Records Covered

The schedule should include:

- Identification and verification documents
- CDD and EDD records
- Beneficial-ownership information
- Customer risk assessments
- Screening alerts and dispositions
- Transaction-monitoring alerts
- Investigation files
- Escalation and reporting decisions
- Training records
- Policy approvals
- Independent-review reports
- Remediation evidence

### Record Standards

Records should be:

- Complete
- Accurate
- Secure
- Searchable
- Accessible to authorised staff
- Protected against unauthorised alteration
- Retained and destroyed according to approved requirements

## 17. Training

### Recommendation

Implement a documented AML and sanctions training programme.

### Training Groups

Training should be tailored for:

- All employees
- KYC analysts
- Transaction-monitoring analysts
- Customer-support staff
- Operations teams
- Product and technology staff
- Senior management
- Board members where appropriate

### Programme Requirements

The programme should include:

- Onboarding training
- Annual refresher training
- Role-specific training
- Regulatory and typology updates
- Knowledge testing
- Attendance records
- Remediation for failed assessments

## 18. Quality Assurance

### Recommendation

Create a risk-based quality-assurance programme.

### Review Areas

QA should include samples of:

- KYC files
- Risk ratings
- EDD files
- Screening dispositions
- Transaction-monitoring alerts
- Escalated investigations
- Case narratives
- Recordkeeping

### Reporting

QA findings should identify:

- Error type
- Severity
- Root cause
- Responsible team
- Corrective action
- Target completion date
- Validation result

## 19. Independent Testing

### Recommendation

Require periodic independent testing of the AML programme.

FinCEN describes independent review as an assessment of whether the MSB’s AML programme is adequate, compliant with applicable BSA obligations and operating consistently with its own policies and procedures. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

### Scope

Testing should assess:

- Policy adequacy
- Risk assessment
- Customer controls
- Sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Investigations
- Reporting governance
- Training
- Recordkeeping
- Vendor oversight
- Remediation of prior findings

### Reviewer Independence

The review should not be conducted by the person who designed or operates the controls being tested.

## 20. Third-Party Vendor Oversight

### Recommendation

Establish a formal compliance-vendor management framework.

### Lifecycle Controls

The framework should cover:

- Pre-contract due diligence
- Assessment of technical capability
- Data sources and coverage
- Contractual responsibilities
- Service levels
- Data protection
- Model or rule changes
- Incident notification
- Performance monitoring
- Periodic testing
- Business continuity
- Exit planning

NorthBridge Pay remains responsible for overseeing compliance processes even where technology or operational tasks are outsourced.

## 21. Management Reporting

### Recommendation

Introduce a regular compliance-management information pack.

### Suggested Metrics

Reports should include:

- New customer volumes
- Verification failures
- Customer risk distribution
- High-risk customer approvals
- PEP and sanctions alerts
- Screening backlogs
- Transaction-monitoring alerts
- Open investigations
- Escalation decisions
- Reporting decisions
- Alert ageing
- Training completion
- QA error rates
- Independent-review findings
- Overdue remediation

## 22. Regulatory Change Management

### Recommendation

Create a process for identifying and implementing relevant regulatory developments.

### Process

The company should:

1. Monitor relevant official sources
2. Record identified developments
3. Assess applicability
4. Determine policy and process impact
5. Assign an implementation owner
6. Track completion
7. Report material changes to management
8. Retain evidence of implementation

## 23. Remediation Governance

### Recommendation

Maintain a central remediation tracker.

### Minimum Fields

The tracker should record:

- Finding reference
- Description
- Severity
- Corrective action
- Responsible owner
- Target date
- Current status
- Supporting evidence
- Validation result
- Closure approval

Critical and overdue actions should be reported to senior management.

## 24. Recommended Implementation Timeline

| Phase | Main Actions | Illustrative Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Risk assessment, governance, customer-risk model, sanctions, monitoring, investigations and reporting governance | 0–30 days |
| Phase 2 | CDD, EDD, beneficial ownership, PEP controls and ongoing review | 30–60 days |
| Phase 3 | Recordkeeping, training, QA, vendor oversight and management reporting | 60–90 days |
| Phase 4 | Policy approval, implementation testing and independent review | 90–120 days |

The timing is illustrative and should be adjusted according to risk, resources and applicable requirements.

## 25. Final Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should not rely on policy redrafting alone.

The company should implement supporting procedures, templates, systems, training, monitoring, quality assurance and independent testing.

The AML policy should only be presented as a mature framework once:

- Critical gaps have been remediated
- Responsibilities have been assigned
- Controls have been implemented
- Staff have been trained
- Evidence of operation is available
- Remaining risks have been accepted by appropriate management
