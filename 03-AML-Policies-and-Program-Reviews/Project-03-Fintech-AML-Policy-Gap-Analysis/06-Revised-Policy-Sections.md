# Revised AML Policy Sections

## 1. Purpose and Status of This Document

This document contains selected revised sections for the fictional NorthBridge Pay, Inc. AML policy.

The revised wording addresses several high-priority weaknesses identified during the policy gap analysis.

It is intended to demonstrate how broad or optional policy statements may be converted into clearer, risk-based and operational requirements.

This is not a complete AML policy.

---

## 2. AML Governance and Oversight

### 2.1 Governing Authority

NorthBridge Pay’s governing authority and senior management are responsible for promoting an effective culture of AML, sanctions and financial-crime compliance.

They must:

- Approve the AML policy and material amendments
- Receive regular reports on material financial-crime risks
- Review significant control weaknesses and overdue remediation
- Ensure that the AML programme receives sufficient staffing, systems and resources
- Support the authority and independence of the Chief Compliance Officer
- Review material findings from quality assurance and independent testing

### 2.2 Chief Compliance Officer

NorthBridge Pay will appoint a suitably qualified Chief Compliance Officer with responsibility for the design, implementation, maintenance and oversight of the AML programme.

The Chief Compliance Officer must have:

- Sufficient authority and independence
- Direct access to senior management
- Access to relevant customer, account, transaction and system information
- Authority to escalate material compliance concerns
- Authority to require remediation
- Sufficient resources to perform the role effectively

The Chief Compliance Officer is responsible for:

- Maintaining the AML policy and supporting procedures
- Overseeing customer due diligence and risk assessment
- Overseeing sanctions and PEP screening
- Overseeing transaction monitoring and investigations
- Reviewing material internal escalations
- Governing applicable suspicious-activity reporting decisions
- Coordinating training, quality assurance and independent testing
- Reporting material issues to senior management and the governing authority
- Tracking remediation to completion

Delegation of tasks does not remove the Chief Compliance Officer’s oversight responsibility.

### 2.3 Management Reporting

The Chief Compliance Officer must provide compliance reports at least quarterly.

Reports should include, where relevant:

- Customer onboarding volumes
- Verification failures
- Customer risk distribution
- High-risk customer approvals
- Sanctions and PEP alerts
- Transaction-monitoring alerts and ageing
- Open and escalated investigations
- Suspicious-activity reporting decisions
- Quality-assurance findings
- Training completion
- Independent-testing findings
- Material incidents
- Overdue remediation

Material issues must be reported promptly and must not wait for the next scheduled report.

## 3. Enterprise AML Risk Assessment

NorthBridge Pay must maintain a documented enterprise-wide AML risk assessment.

The assessment must consider the company’s:

- Customer types
- Products and services
- Transaction types
- Geographic exposure
- Delivery channels
- Payment methods
- Cross-border activity
- Crypto-related exposure
- Third-party service providers
- Known financial-crime typologies

For each risk area, the assessment must identify:

1. Inherent risk
2. Relevant mitigating controls
3. Control effectiveness
4. Residual risk
5. Required remediation or risk acceptance

The assessment must be approved by senior management.

It must be reviewed:

- At least annually
- Before launching a material new product
- Before entering a new jurisdiction
- When introducing a new payment or crypto-related service
- Following a material control failure
- Following a significant regulatory or business-model change
- When new typologies materially affect the company’s risk exposure

The AML programme, customer-risk methodology, monitoring rules and training programme must reflect the findings of the risk assessment.

## 4. Customer Identification and Verification

### 4.1 General Requirement

NorthBridge Pay must obtain and verify required customer information before account activation.

No account may be activated where required verification remains incomplete, failed or materially inconsistent, unless a formally approved exception is permitted by applicable policy and law.

### 4.2 Individual Customers

NorthBridge Pay must obtain, at minimum:

- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Nationality or country of residence, where relevant
- Contact details
- Government-issued identification
- Tax-identification information where required

Verification must use reliable and independent sources or approved verification technology.

### 4.3 Business Customers

For business customers, NorthBridge Pay must obtain, at minimum:

- Legal business name
- Trading name, where applicable
- Registration or formation number
- Jurisdiction of formation
- Registered and operating address
- Tax-identification information
- Nature of business
- Purpose of the account
- Ownership and control information
- Information regarding authorised representatives
- Expected transaction profile

Business existence and registration must be verified using reliable and independent records.

### 4.4 Failed or Inconclusive Verification

Failed, incomplete or inconclusive verification must be referred for manual review.

The reviewer must:

- Identify the reason for the failure
- Request additional evidence where appropriate
- Review inconsistencies
- Document the outcome
- Determine whether escalation is required

Material unresolved discrepancies must result in rejection, restriction or escalation before account activation.

## 5. Customer Due Diligence

NorthBridge Pay must complete and document customer due diligence before establishing a relationship.

CDD must establish:

- The customer’s identity
- The nature and purpose of the relationship
- The customer’s occupation or business activity
- The intended use of the account
- Expected transaction types
- Expected values and frequency
- Expected jurisdictions
- Expected counterparties
- Relevant ownership and control information
- Source of funds where required
- The initial customer risk rating

CDD information must be sufficiently detailed to allow future account activity to be compared with the expected customer profile.

The analyst must record:

- Information reviewed
- Documents obtained
- Any discrepancies
- Screening results
- Risk factors
- Final recommendation
- Required approval

Accounts must not be approved where the customer’s identity, ownership, account purpose or expected activity cannot be reasonably understood.

## 6. Customer Risk Assessment

### 6.1 Risk Factors

Each customer must receive a documented risk assessment considering, where relevant:

- Customer type
- Occupation or industry
- Products and services used
- Geographic exposure
- Expected transaction profile
- Delivery channel
- Ownership complexity
- PEP exposure
- Sanctions exposure
- Adverse media
- Source-of-funds concerns
- Crypto-related exposure
- Prior unusual activity

### 6.2 Risk Categories

Customers must be classified as:

- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk
- Prohibited or Outside Risk Appetite

### 6.3 Documentation and Approval

The final rating must be supported by recorded reasoning.

Manual overrides must include:

- The original system rating
- The revised rating
- The reason for the override
- The approving person
- The approval date

High-risk customers require approval from the Chief Compliance Officer or an authorised senior compliance decision-maker before onboarding.

### 6.4 Review Frequency

Customer risk ratings must be reviewed:

- At least every three years for low-risk customers
- At least every two years for medium-risk customers
- At least annually for high-risk customers
- Whenever a material trigger event occurs

These intervals are internal policy standards and may be shortened according to risk or applicable requirements.

## 7. Enhanced Due Diligence

EDD must be applied where the customer or relationship presents elevated risk.

Possible EDD triggers include:

- PEP exposure
- Higher-risk jurisdictions
- Complex or opaque ownership
- Material adverse media
- Significant crypto-related activity
- Unexplained third-party funding
- High-value or unusual cross-border activity
- Source-of-funds inconsistencies
- Activity materially inconsistent with the customer profile
- High-risk industries
- Unresolved verification concerns

EDD measures may include:

- Additional identification and verification
- Independent business and ownership research
- Source-of-funds evidence
- Source-of-wealth evidence
- Adverse-media research
- Additional counterparty information
- Senior-management approval
- Enhanced transaction monitoring
- Lower review thresholds
- More frequent periodic review

The analyst must document:

- The EDD trigger
- Additional information obtained
- Evidence reviewed
- Remaining concerns
- Final recommendation
- Approval decision
- Ongoing control requirements

A high-risk relationship must not be approved until required EDD has been completed satisfactorily.

## 8. Beneficial Ownership

For business customers, NorthBridge Pay must identify and verify relevant natural persons who directly or indirectly own or control the entity.

The review must consider:

- Direct ownership
- Indirect ownership
- Ownership through other entities
- Voting rights
- Control through management or other means
- Nominee arrangements
- Trusts or similar legal structures
- Complex or layered ownership

NorthBridge Pay must establish an internal ownership threshold appropriate to its legal obligations, sponsor-bank requirements and risk appetite.

Where no individual meets the ownership threshold, the company must identify the relevant controlling person according to approved procedures.

Ownership information must be compared with reliable records and supporting documents.

Unresolved ownership discrepancies must be escalated before onboarding.

## 9. Sanctions Screening

### 9.1 Screening Requirements

NorthBridge Pay must apply risk-based sanctions screening to relevant customers, beneficial owners, authorised representatives, counterparties and transactions.

Screening must occur:

- During onboarding
- Before account activation
- When relevant customer information changes
- When sanctions data is updated
- During ongoing rescreening
- Before or during relevant transaction processing
- When new related parties are identified

### 9.2 Alert Review

Sanctions alerts must be reviewed promptly.

The reviewer must compare relevant identifiers, which may include:

- Full name
- Aliases
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Address
- Identification information
- Entity registration details
- Ownership information
- Geographic connections

### 9.3 Disposition

Every alert must have a documented outcome.

A false-positive disposition must explain why the screened party is not the listed person or entity.

A possible true match must be escalated immediately to the Chief Compliance Officer or designated sanctions specialist.

No employee may release or process activity involving an unresolved possible sanctions match without authorised review.

### 9.4 Programme Oversight

NorthBridge Pay must periodically assess whether its screening controls remain appropriate for its risks.

This should include review of:

- List coverage
- Data quality
- Name-matching settings
- Alias detection
- Alert volumes
- False-positive rates
- Escalation quality
- Vendor performance
- Testing results

## 10. Politically Exposed Persons

NorthBridge Pay must maintain procedures for identifying relevant PEPs, their family members and known close associates.

PEP status does not automatically require rejection.

The relationship must be assessed according to:

- The customer’s role and level of influence
- Jurisdiction
- Product use
- Expected activity
- Source of funds
- Source of wealth
- Adverse media
- Other relevant risk factors

Higher-risk PEP relationships may require:

- Enhanced due diligence
- Source-of-funds and source-of-wealth review
- Senior approval
- Enhanced monitoring
- More frequent review
- Documented acceptance rationale

PEP status and related risk must be reviewed throughout the relationship.

## 11. Transaction Monitoring

### 11.1 Monitoring Framework

NorthBridge Pay must maintain transaction-monitoring controls reasonably designed around its customer, product, geographic, payment and crypto-related risks.

Monitoring may include automated rules, behavioural analysis and manual referrals.

### 11.2 Rule Documentation

Each monitoring rule must have documented:

- Purpose
- Relevant risk or typology
- Covered products and customers
- Threshold or parameters
- Required data inputs
- Alert frequency
- Investigation steps
- Escalation criteria
- Rule owner
- Review frequency

### 11.3 Alert Investigation

Analysts must:

1. Review the triggering activity
2. Review the customer profile and risk rating
3. Compare actual and expected activity
4. Review relevant transaction history
5. Identify counterparties and jurisdictions
6. Review prior alerts and cases
7. Obtain supporting information where appropriate
8. Document red flags and possible explanations
9. Decide whether to close or escalate
10. Record the reasoning and reviewer approval

### 11.4 Monitoring Effectiveness

NorthBridge Pay must periodically review:

- Alert volumes
- False-positive rates
- Backlogs
- Alert ageing
- Escalation rates
- Rule coverage
- Data completeness
- Investigator quality
- Emerging typologies

Monitoring rules must be tuned or amended where evidence indicates that they are ineffective or disproportionate.

## 12. Suspicious-Activity Investigation and Escalation

### 12.1 Internal Escalation

Potentially suspicious activity must be escalated through the approved case-management process.

Each case must include:

- Customer information
- Triggering activity
- Review period
- Transaction summary
- Relevant red flags
- Evidence reviewed
- Screening results
- Customer explanation, where appropriate
- Analyst conclusion
- Recommended action
- Reviewer decision

### 12.2 Investigation Timelines

Cases must be handled within internal timelines based on severity.

High-risk matters, sanctions concerns and material reporting risks must receive urgent attention.

Delays must be documented and escalated.

### 12.3 Decision-Making

The decision-maker must determine whether:

- The activity is reasonably explained
- Additional information is required
- Enhanced monitoring is appropriate
- The customer risk rating should change
- Account restriction or exit should be considered
- External reporting obligations may arise

No conclusion of criminal conduct is required before escalation.

## 13. Suspicious Activity Reporting Governance

Where NorthBridge Pay is subject to applicable suspicious-activity reporting requirements, it must maintain a documented reporting procedure.

Certain MSBs, including money transmitters, are subject to FinCEN SAR requirements. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The procedure must address:

- Reporting-decision authority
- Applicable reporting criteria and thresholds
- Filing timelines
- Quality review
- Electronic submission
- Confidentiality
- Supporting-document retention
- Continuing-activity review
- Documentation of decisions not to report

Internal escalation does not automatically mean that an external report will be filed.

Every reporting or non-reporting decision must be documented sufficiently to explain the reasoning.

Employees must not disclose the existence or possible filing of a suspicious activity report to the customer or unauthorised persons.

## 14. Recordkeeping

NorthBridge Pay must maintain complete, accurate, secure and retrievable compliance records.

Records must include, where relevant:

- Identification and verification documents
- CDD and EDD records
- Beneficial-ownership information
- Customer risk assessments
- Screening alerts and dispositions
- Transaction-monitoring alerts
- Investigation records
- Escalation decisions
- Regulatory reports
- Training records
- Policy approvals
- Quality-assurance results
- Independent-review reports
- Remediation evidence

The company must maintain a formal retention schedule based on applicable legal, regulatory, contractual and business requirements.

Access must be restricted to authorised persons.

Records must be protected against unauthorised alteration, deletion or disclosure.

---

## 15. Training

NorthBridge Pay must provide AML and sanctions training appropriate to each employee’s responsibilities.

Training must include:

- Initial onboarding training
- Recurring refresher training
- Role-specific training
- Updates following material policy or regulatory changes
- Relevant financial-crime typologies
- Escalation and confidentiality requirements
- Knowledge testing where appropriate

Training completion must be documented.

Employees who fail required assessments must receive follow-up training.

Senior management must receive training appropriate to its oversight responsibilities.

---

## 16. Independent Testing

NorthBridge Pay must arrange periodic independent testing of the AML programme.

FinCEN explains that independent review should assess whether an MSB’s programme is adequate, complies with applicable BSA requirements and operates consistently with its own policies and procedures. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

The review must be performed by a suitably knowledgeable person who is independent of the activities being tested.

The scope should include, where relevant:

- Risk assessment
- Governance
- Customer identification
- CDD and EDD
- Beneficial ownership
- Sanctions and PEP screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Investigations
- Reporting governance
- Recordkeeping
- Training
- Vendor oversight
- Quality assurance
- Prior remediation

Findings must be:

- Assigned a severity
- Reported to management
- Given an accountable owner
- Assigned a target date
- Tracked to completion
- Independently validated before closure

## 17. Third-Party Vendor Oversight

NorthBridge Pay remains responsible for oversight of compliance activities supported by third-party providers.

Before appointing a material compliance vendor, the company must assess:

- Experience and reputation
- Data sources
- Geographic and product coverage
- Technical capability
- Information-security controls
- Business continuity
- Auditability
- Regulatory or legal concerns
- Subcontracting arrangements

Contracts should address:

- Responsibilities
- Service levels
- Data access
- Record retention
- Incident notification
- System changes
- Testing rights
- Termination support

Vendor performance must be monitored periodically.

Control failures must be escalated and remediated.

---

## 18. Policy Review and Approval

The AML policy must be reviewed:

- At least annually
- Following material regulatory changes
- Following significant business-model changes
- Following new product or geographic expansion
- Following material control failures
- Following significant independent-review findings

Each version must record:

- Version number
- Effective date
- Policy owner
- Summary of changes
- Approval authority
- Approval date

Material changes require formal approval by the appropriate governing authority or senior-management body.
