# Original AML Policy

## Document Information

**Company:** NorthBridge Pay, Inc.  
**Policy owner:** Chief Compliance Officer  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** January 2025  
**Last reviewed:** January 2025  
**Approval authority:** Senior Management  

## 1. Purpose

NorthBridge Pay, Inc. is committed to preventing money laundering, terrorist financing, fraud and other illegal activity.

The company will comply with applicable anti-money laundering laws and will take reasonable steps to prevent its products and services from being used for unlawful purposes.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all NorthBridge Pay employees, contractors, customers and business activities.

Employees are expected to comply with this policy and report suspicious conduct to the Chief Compliance Officer.

## 3. Compliance Responsibility

The Chief Compliance Officer is responsible for overseeing the company’s AML program.

The Chief Compliance Officer may delegate compliance tasks to employees where appropriate.

All employees are responsible for supporting the company’s AML obligations.

## 4. Customer Identification

NorthBridge Pay will collect identifying information from all customers.

Individual customers must provide:

- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Contact details
- Government-issued identification

Business customers must provide:

- Legal business name
- Business address
- Registration information
- Tax identification information
- Ownership information

The company may use third-party providers to verify customer information.

Accounts should not normally be opened where the customer’s identity cannot be verified.

## 5. Customer Due Diligence

NorthBridge Pay will conduct customer due diligence before providing services.

Customer due diligence may include:

- Understanding the customer’s identity
- Understanding the nature of the customer’s business
- Establishing the intended purpose of the account
- Reviewing ownership information
- Considering expected transaction activity

Customers presenting increased risk may be subject to additional checks.

The compliance team may request further documentation where necessary.

## 6. Beneficial Ownership

NorthBridge Pay will obtain beneficial-ownership information from business customers.

The company will seek to identify the individuals who own or control the business.

Additional information may be requested where the ownership structure appears complex.

## 7. Customer Risk Assessment

Customers may be classified according to their level of money-laundering risk.

Risk factors may include:

- Customer type
- Business activity
- Geographic location
- Transaction volume
- Products used
- Delivery channel

Higher-risk customers may require additional review.

## 8. Enhanced Due Diligence

Enhanced due diligence may be applied where a customer presents increased risk.

Enhanced due diligence may include:

- Obtaining additional identification
- Requesting source-of-funds information
- Reviewing source of wealth
- Conducting additional internet searches
- Obtaining management approval

The compliance team will determine when enhanced due diligence is appropriate.

## 9. Sanctions Screening

NorthBridge Pay will screen customers against applicable sanctions lists.

Screening may be conducted through a third-party provider.

Potential sanctions matches must be referred to the Chief Compliance Officer.

The company will not knowingly conduct prohibited transactions.

## 10. Politically Exposed Persons

NorthBridge Pay may identify whether customers are politically exposed persons.

PEPs may be subject to additional due diligence and management review.

The company may decline a relationship where the risk is considered unacceptable.

## 11. Transaction Monitoring

NorthBridge Pay will monitor customer transactions for potentially unusual or suspicious activity.

Monitoring may include automated systems and manual review.

Examples of activity that may require investigation include:

- Transactions inconsistent with the customer profile
- Rapid movement of funds
- Large or unusual transfers
- Multiple third-party payments
- Transactions involving higher-risk jurisdictions
- Repeated cryptocurrency-related transactions

Compliance employees will review alerts and may request supporting information from customers.

## 12. Suspicious Activity

Employees must report potentially suspicious activity to the Chief Compliance Officer.

The Chief Compliance Officer will decide whether further investigation or reporting is required.

Employees must not inform customers that suspicious activity has been reported or is under review.

The company will cooperate with lawful information requests from regulators and law-enforcement authorities.

## 13. Recordkeeping

NorthBridge Pay will retain compliance records for an appropriate period.

Records may include:

- Customer identification information
- Due diligence documents
- Screening results
- Transaction-monitoring alerts
- Investigation records
- Regulatory reports
- Training records

Records must be stored securely and remain accessible to authorised personnel.

## 14. Training

Employees will receive AML training when they join the company.

Additional training may be provided where needed.

Employees working in compliance-related roles should understand the requirements relevant to their responsibilities.

## 15. Independent Review

The company may arrange for an independent review of its AML program when considered appropriate.

The review may be performed by an internal employee or an external consultant.

Any identified weaknesses should be reported to management.

## 16. Third-Party Providers

NorthBridge Pay may rely on third-party providers for:

- Identity verification
- Sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Payment processing
- Banking services

The company will seek to use reputable service providers.

Responsibility for monitoring service-provider performance will be shared between compliance and operations.

## 17. Policy Breaches

Employees who fail to comply with this policy may be subject to disciplinary action.

Material breaches must be reported to senior management.

## 18. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed periodically and updated where necessary.

Changes may be approved by the Chief Compliance Officer or senior management.
