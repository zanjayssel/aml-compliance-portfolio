# Client Scenario

## 1. Company Overview

**Company name:** NorthBridge Pay, Inc.  
**Jurisdiction of incorporation:** Delaware, United States  
**Business type:** Early-stage fintech and money-services business  
**Operating model:** Non-bank fintech operating through a sponsor bank and third-party payment processor  
**Primary customers:** U.S. individuals, freelancers, small businesses and online merchants  
**Primary products:** Digital payment accounts, domestic transfers, cross-border payments and limited fiat-to-crypto brokerage access  

NorthBridge Pay, Inc. is a fictional U.S.-based fintech company offering digital payment services to individuals and small businesses.

The company provides customers with access to digital payment accounts, domestic transfers, cross-border transactions and limited crypto-related services through external banking, payment-processing and identity-verification partners.

NorthBridge Pay does not operate as a traditional bank. Its customer accounts and payment services are supported through a sponsor-bank relationship and third-party processing arrangements.

## 2. Business Growth

NorthBridge Pay launched 18 months ago and has grown rapidly.

The company currently serves approximately:

- 12,000 individual customers
- 850 small-business customers
- 120 online merchants

The company processes approximately USD 9 million in monthly transaction activity.

Growth has been driven by:

- Remote freelancers receiving cross-border payments
- Small businesses paying overseas suppliers
- Online merchants accepting digital payments
- Customers transferring funds between the United States and selected foreign jurisdictions
- Customers using the platform to purchase or sell limited amounts of cryptocurrency through an external provider

## 3. Geographic Exposure

NorthBridge Pay primarily serves customers based in the United States.

However, customer transactions may involve:

- Canada
- Mexico
- the United Kingdom
- the European Union
- South Africa
- Nigeria
- the United Arab Emirates
- selected Latin American jurisdictions

The company has not yet completed a formal geographic-risk assessment.

Some higher-risk countries are blocked by the payment processor, but NorthBridge Pay has no documented internal methodology for rating geographic risk.

## 4. Customer Types

NorthBridge Pay provides services to:

### Individual Customers

Individual customers may use the platform for:

- Personal transfers
- Receiving freelance income
- Paying family members
- Cross-border remittances
- Limited cryptocurrency transactions

### Business Customers

Business customers may use the platform for:

- Supplier payments
- Merchant settlements
- Payroll-related payments
- Cross-border business transfers
- Online sales collections

The company does not currently serve large financial institutions or publicly listed companies.

## 5. Compliance Structure

NorthBridge Pay has appointed a Chief Compliance Officer.

The compliance team currently consists of:

- One Chief Compliance Officer
- Two KYC analysts
- One transaction-monitoring analyst
- One operations employee supporting compliance administration

The compliance team reports to senior management.

The board receives limited compliance updates, but there is no formal reporting schedule or documented board-oversight framework.

## 6. Current AML Program

NorthBridge Pay has a written AML policy.

The policy is six pages long and was drafted before the company launched.

The policy includes brief references to:

- Customer identification
- Sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Suspicious activity
- Recordkeeping
- Employee training

However, the policy does not explain in sufficient detail how these controls should operate.

Management has asked for the policy to be reviewed before it is shared with:

- The sponsor bank
- The payment processor
- Potential investors
- External compliance consultants
- Business partners

## 7. Existing Compliance Processes

### Customer Identification

Customers are required to provide:

- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address
- Government-issued identification
- Contact details

Business customers are also required to provide:

- Business name
- Registration documents
- Business address
- Ownership information
- Tax identification information

Identity verification is conducted through a third-party provider.

The policy does not explain how failed or inconclusive verification results must be handled.

### Customer Due Diligence

The company collects basic customer information.

However:

- There is no formal customer-risk scoring model
- Customers are not consistently classified as low, medium or high risk
- Risk factors are not weighted
- There is no documented customer-acceptance framework
- Periodic-review requirements are unclear

### Enhanced Due Diligence

The policy refers to enhanced due diligence but does not define:

- Which customers require EDD
- Which documents must be collected
- Who must approve high-risk customers
- How source of funds should be reviewed
- How source of wealth should be assessed
- How often high-risk customers should be reviewed

### Beneficial Ownership

Business customers are asked to provide ownership information.

However:

- The ownership threshold is not clearly defined
- Indirect ownership is not addressed
- Control through other means is not addressed
- Complex ownership structures are not adequately covered
- Independent verification requirements are unclear

### Sanctions and PEP Screening

Customers are screened using a third-party screening tool.

The current policy does not clearly explain:

- When screening must occur
- How often screening must be refreshed
- How alerts must be reviewed
- How false positives must be documented
- When potential matches must be escalated
- How family members and close associates of PEPs should be handled

### Transaction Monitoring

The company uses a rules-based monitoring system.

Examples of alerts include:

- Transactions above internal value thresholds
- Rapid movement of funds
- Multiple third-party transfers
- Unusual cross-border activity
- Activity inconsistent with the customer profile
- Repeated transactions involving crypto-related counterparties

The policy does not document:

- The monitoring rules
- The reason for the selected thresholds
- Alert-investigation standards
- Case-escalation requirements
- Quality-assurance procedures
- How monitoring rules are reviewed and updated

### Suspicious Activity Escalation

Analysts may escalate unusual activity to the Chief Compliance Officer.

However:

- There is no formal escalation form
- Decision-making responsibilities are unclear
- Investigation timelines are not defined
- Case narratives are not standardised
- Management reporting is inconsistent
- The process for determining whether external reporting may be required is not clearly documented

### Recordkeeping

Compliance records are stored in shared cloud folders and internal systems.

The company has no clear records-retention schedule covering:

- Customer identification records
- Screening results
- Risk assessments
- Transaction-monitoring alerts
- Case files
- Escalation decisions
- Training records
- Policy approvals

### Training

Employees receive general compliance training during onboarding.

There is no documented annual training schedule.

Role-specific training for KYC analysts, transaction-monitoring analysts and senior management has not been formally established.

### Independent Testing

NorthBridge Pay has not yet completed an independent AML-program review.

There is no documented schedule for independent testing, internal audit or compliance-quality assurance.

## 8. Trigger for the Review

NorthBridge Pay’s sponsor bank has requested additional information about the company’s AML control framework.

The bank has asked management to demonstrate that the company has:

- A clear risk-based AML program
- Defined customer due diligence procedures
- Enhanced due diligence controls
- Effective sanctions and PEP screening
- Transaction-monitoring procedures
- Suspicious-activity escalation processes
- Recordkeeping controls
- Staff training
- Governance and oversight
- Independent testing

Management is concerned that the current policy does not adequately explain how these controls operate.

## 9. Client Request

NorthBridge Pay has requested an independent portfolio-based review of its existing AML policy and control framework.

The review should:

1. Identify material policy and control gaps
2. Assess the seriousness of each gap
3. Explain why the weakness matters
4. Prioritise remediation actions
5. Recommend practical improvements
6. Draft revised policy wording for selected high-priority areas
7. Prepare an executive compliance memo for management

## 10. Scope of the Review

The review will assess the following areas:

- AML governance and accountability
- Customer identification and verification
- Customer Due Diligence
- Enhanced Due Diligence
- Beneficial ownership
- Customer risk assessment
- Sanctions and PEP screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Suspicious-activity escalation
- Recordkeeping
- Staff training
- Quality assurance
- Independent testing
- Third-party vendor oversight
- Policy approval and review

## 11. Review Limitations

This project does not assess:

- State money-transmission licensing
- Tax compliance
- Consumer-protection requirements
- Data-privacy compliance
- Cybersecurity requirements
- Securities regulation
- The legal classification of specific crypto products
- The effectiveness of actual technology systems
- The accuracy of real customer data
- Whether the company satisfies every applicable U.S. legal requirement

## 12. Analyst Role

**Role:** Independent AML Policy Review Analyst — Fictional Portfolio Project

The analyst will review the existing policy, identify weaknesses, assign risk and priority ratings, recommend remediation actions and draft selected revised policy sections.
