# Executive Compliance Memorandum

**To:** Board of Directors and Senior Management  
**From:** Independent AML Policy Review Analyst  
**Company:** NorthBridge Pay, Inc.  
**Subject:** AML Policy Review and Control Gap Assessment  
**Date:** July 2026  
**Classification:** Confidential — Fictional Portfolio Case Study  

## 1. Executive Summary

NorthBridge Pay, Inc. requested a review of its existing Anti-Money Laundering policy and related control framework before presenting the programme to its sponsor bank, payment processor and other external stakeholders.

The review found that the current policy identifies many relevant AML topics but does not translate them into sufficiently clear, mandatory and operational requirements.

The policy contains broad statements concerning customer identification, customer due diligence, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, suspicious activity, recordkeeping, training and independent review. However, many of these provisions use discretionary language and do not define:

- Control ownership
- Required procedures
- Decision-making authority
- Escalation standards
- Review timelines
- Approval requirements
- Documentation standards
- Testing expectations
- Evidence of control effectiveness

The overall conclusion is that the current AML framework requires material remediation before it can reasonably be presented as a mature and fully operational programme.

**Overall programme risk:** High  
**Policy readiness:** Material Remediation Required  
**Recommended management response:** Immediate prioritised remediation

## 3. Background

NorthBridge Pay is a fictional U.S.-based fintech offering:

- Digital payment accounts
- Domestic transfers
- Cross-border payments
- Merchant payment services
- Limited fiat-to-crypto brokerage access

The company operates through a sponsor bank, payment processor and third-party identity-verification and screening providers.

NorthBridge Pay currently processes approximately USD 9 million in monthly transaction activity and serves individuals, freelancers, small businesses and online merchants.

The company has grown rapidly, but its AML policy has not developed at the same pace as its products, customers and geographic exposure.

## 5. Governance Concerns

The current policy assigns general responsibility to the Chief Compliance Officer but does not adequately define:

- The CCO’s authority
- Independence and access to information
- Required reporting to senior management
- Board oversight
- Resource expectations
- Responsibility for remediation
- Escalation of material compliance issues

The absence of formal governance may result in inconsistent accountability and limited senior-management visibility.

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should adopt a documented governance structure that:

- Establishes clear responsibilities
- Gives the CCO sufficient authority and independence
- Requires regular management reporting
- Escalates material issues promptly
- Tracks remediation to completion
- Requires formal policy approval

## 6. Risk Assessment Concerns

NorthBridge Pay has not documented an enterprise-wide AML risk assessment.

As a result, the company cannot clearly demonstrate that its controls are designed around its:

- Customer population
- Products and services
- Cross-border activity
- Geographic exposure
- Payment channels
- Crypto-related services
- Third-party arrangements

The absence of a documented risk assessment also weakens the basis for customer-risk scoring, transaction-monitoring rules and sanctions controls.

### Recommendation

Management should complete an enterprise AML risk assessment as an immediate priority.

The assessment should identify:

- Inherent risks
- Existing controls
- Control effectiveness
- Residual risks
- Required remediation
- Management-approved risk acceptance

## 7. Customer Due Diligence Concerns

The existing policy requires basic customer information but does not create a complete customer-acceptance and risk-classification framework.

Key weaknesses include:

- No minimum CDD standard
- No formal risk-scoring methodology
- Undefined EDD triggers
- Incomplete beneficial-ownership procedures
- No risk-based periodic-review schedule
- Unclear treatment of failed verification
- No clear high-risk customer approval process

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should implement:

- Mandatory customer identification standards
- Documented CDD requirements
- Low-, medium- and high-risk categories
- Defined EDD triggers
- Beneficial-ownership verification procedures
- Senior approval for high-risk customers
- Periodic and event-driven reviews
- Clear customer rejection and exit criteria

## 8. Sanctions and PEP Concerns

The policy states that customers are screened but does not clearly define:

- When screening occurs
- Which parties are screened
- Which lists are covered
- How often customers are rescreened
- How transactions are screened
- How false positives are resolved
- When alerts require escalation
- How possible true matches are handled

OFAC’s compliance framework emphasises management commitment, risk assessment, internal controls, testing and training as important components of a risk-based sanctions compliance programme.  

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should document:

- Onboarding and ongoing screening
- Transaction-screening requirements
- Alert-review standards
- Match-resolution procedures
- Escalation and restriction requirements
- Screening evidence retention
- Vendor performance testing
- PEP definitions and EDD requirements

## 9. Transaction Monitoring Concerns

NorthBridge Pay uses automated monitoring rules but has not documented:

- The risks addressed by each rule
- Threshold rationale
- Customer and product coverage
- Required data inputs
- Alert-review standards
- Escalation requirements
- Quality assurance
- Rule tuning and validation

Without this documentation, the company may be unable to demonstrate that its monitoring framework is reasonably designed around its actual risk exposure.

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should create a transaction-monitoring governance document that records:

- Rule purpose
- Typology addressed
- Thresholds and parameters
- Investigation procedures
- Case-escalation requirements
- Alert ageing standards
- Quality controls
- Rule-performance monitoring
- Tuning and validation

## 10. Suspicious-Activity Investigation Concerns

The current policy permits analysts to escalate concerns to the CCO but does not define a formal case-management process.

The policy lacks:

- Investigation stages
- Case ownership
- Internal timelines
- Evidence requirements
- Standardised narratives
- Escalation thresholds
- Closure criteria
- Reviewer approval

Certain MSBs, including money transmitters, are subject to FinCEN suspicious-activity reporting requirements.  

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should create a documented procedure covering:

1. Alert or referral intake
2. Initial review
3. Evidence collection
4. Customer-profile comparison
5. Transaction analysis
6. Counterparty screening
7. Customer clarification
8. Internal escalation
9. Reporting decision
10. Case closure and retention

The procedure should distinguish clearly between:

- Internal escalation
- Enhanced investigation
- External reporting
- Account restriction
- Relationship exit

## 11. Recordkeeping Concerns

The current policy requires records to be kept for an “appropriate period” but provides no formal retention schedule.

This may result in:

- Inconsistent retention
- Missing evidence
- Unclear ownership
- Inability to reproduce prior decisions
- Weak audit readiness

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should adopt a documented retention schedule covering:

- Identification records
- CDD and EDD files
- Risk assessments
- Screening alerts
- Transaction-monitoring cases
- Investigation records
- Reporting decisions
- Training records
- Independent-review reports
- Remediation evidence

## 12. Training and Quality Assurance Concerns

Training is currently described as an onboarding activity, with additional training provided only where needed.

The policy does not require:

- Annual refresher training
- Role-specific training
- Senior-management training
- Knowledge testing
- Training remediation
- Formal quality assurance

### Recommendation

Management should implement:

- Mandatory onboarding training
- Annual refresher training
- Role-specific training
- Typology updates
- Completion tracking
- Knowledge assessment
- Remedial training
- Quality review of KYC, screening, monitoring and investigation files

## 13. Independent Testing Concerns

The current policy states that independent testing may occur when management considers it appropriate.

This wording makes testing appear optional.

FinCEN explains that independent review is intended to assess the adequacy of an MSB’s AML programme and whether the business is operating consistently with BSA requirements and its own policies and procedures.  

### Recommendation

NorthBridge Pay should establish a periodic independent-testing programme that:

- Uses a suitably knowledgeable and independent reviewer
- Covers policy design and operational effectiveness
- Reports findings to senior management
- Assigns accountable action owners
- Tracks remediation
- Independently validates closure

## 14. Third-Party Vendor Concerns

NorthBridge Pay relies on third parties for:

- Identity verification
- Sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Payment processing
- Banking services

The policy does not adequately define:

- Vendor due diligence
- Contractual responsibilities
- Service-level monitoring
- Data-quality testing
- Incident escalation
- Business continuity
- Exit planning

### Recommendation

The company should create a formal vendor-governance framework covering:

- Pre-contract review
- Technical capability
- Data coverage
- Auditability
- Service levels
- Ongoing testing
- Incident reporting
- Contingency planning
- Termination and migration support

Outsourcing technology or operational functions does not remove the company’s responsibility to oversee the relevant controls.

## 16. Sponsor-Bank Readiness

The current policy should not be presented as a fully mature AML control framework without further remediation.

Before submission, management should be able to demonstrate:

- Formal approval
- Documented procedures
- Assigned responsibilities
- Operational evidence
- Training completion
- Quality controls
- Testing results
- Remediation tracking
- Management oversight

A revised policy alone will not be sufficient if the supporting controls have not been implemented.

## 17. Final Opinion

The NorthBridge Pay AML policy provides a basic foundation but is not sufficiently detailed to support consistent implementation or demonstrate a mature risk-based programme.

The most significant weaknesses affect:

- Risk assessment
- Customer classification
- Sanctions screening
- Transaction monitoring
- Suspicious-activity investigation
- Reporting governance
- Independent testing

The recommended outcome is:

**Material Remediation Required**

Management should prioritise the critical findings, implement supporting procedures and controls, train responsible personnel and independently test the programme before presenting it as a mature AML framework.
